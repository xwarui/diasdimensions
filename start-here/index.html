Developmental Sequences

Operators unfold in mandatory order — not by convention, but by logical necessity. You cannot relate before you distinguish. You cannot reflect before you have content to reflect upon.

2–4 Perceptual foundations — Distinguish, relate, stabilize. The basic capacity to have a world.
5–6 Active engagement — Categorize, sustain. The system begins to act on what it perceives.
7–9 Reflective organization — Meta-process, bind across levels, resolve. The system becomes aware of itself.

In consciousness research, this maps directly to brain architecture: posterior cortex handles basic content (Operators 2–6), frontal regions handle reflection (Operator 7), interareal synchrony handles cross-level binding (Operator 8). The sequence isn’t imposed on the brain — it emerges from the logic of orientation capacity.
