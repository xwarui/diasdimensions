The Foundation of Origin

The grounded square

The forward run discovers this rule at Step 4: CCA. The chapter’s thesis: the distinction-rule applied to its own prior output produces a grounded square. One application produces two states. Applied to its own result, it produces four — arranged as corners of a square with an interior.

§4.1 Foundation at Depth

At depth 2, the digit 2 mirroring itself (22) is the distinction-rule applied to its own output. The first application takes an undivided state and introduces two sides. The second application takes the two sides and applies the same rule to them — producing two levels: an object level and a structural level. The two-level structure is Foundation’s depth-register home.

§4.2 Foundation at Position

The binary address-map applies the binary distinction twice, producing four corners of a square: (0/0), (0/1), (1/0), (1/1). The framework’s four named functions — Container, Build, Read, Constrain — are the C-verb slice of this map. The four-node binary square is what two applications of the binary distinction produce.

§4.3 One Rule Across Registers

At depth, 22 is two levels held simultaneously. At position, the binary address-map is four corners of a square. Both: two applications of the distinction-rule produce a four-position structure with an enclosed interior.

§4.4 The Grammatical Nature of Foundation

4 is the first composite: 4 = 2 × 2. Not a new irreducible rule but the first construction from what the grammar already has. 4 is the first perfect square. The four positions tile exactly into a 2×2 grid. 4 is the minimum for a closed figure that is also a grid.

§4.5 The Stabilization

The stabilized capability is Ω₄. What it IS is the rule of grounded distinction. What it DOES is the producing of enclosed four-position structure.

§4.6 The Handoff

The grammar now has rules for distinguishing (Ω₂), connecting (Ω₃), and grounding (Ω₄). What it does not yet have is a rule for acting — for running through the structure it has built. The Action of Origin picks up at the first complete operation the grammar performs.

End of Chapter 4 — The Foundation of Origin.
