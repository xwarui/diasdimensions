Origin of Orientation

The Action of Origin

Direction is the action

Chapter 4 established the first composite — the mirror applied to itself. Chapter 5 returns to a prime. 5 is irreducible. The grammar cannot construct Action from what it already has; it finds it.

The chapter’s thesis: a traversal has direction, and that direction is the action. The inner doors are directed traversals. OCA runs from O, through C, to A. The direction is not a property added after the fact. The direction is what the traversal is.

§5.1 Action at Depth

At depth 2, 55 is action applied to action — traversal whose input is the output of a prior traversal. The output feeds forward. This is the iteration structure: each run takes the last result and traverses it again.

§5.2 Action at Position

Each inner door runs from its subject position through its verb position to its object position. OCA and OAC begin from the same subject (O) but run to different destinations through different verbs. These are not the same traversal read forward and backward. They are two distinct acts. Direction is constitutive — it cannot be separated from the act. The six inner doors are the grammar’s complete inventory of directed acts.

§5.3 One Rule Across Registers

Both registers carry the same content: action has direction, and the direction is the act. Reversing gives a different operation, not the same operation reversed.

§5.4 The Grammatical Nature of Action

5 is the first prime after the first composite. After 4 = 2², irreducibility returns. 5 generates no composite within the operator range (5×2=10, outside 2–9). 5 cannot tile — the crystallographic restriction excludes period 5 from periodic structures.

§5.5 The Stabilization

The stabilized capability is Ω₅. What it IS is the rule of directed traversal. What it DOES is the running of a traversal that produces output not present before the run.

§5.6 The Handoff

Action produces output. What the grammar does not yet have is a rule for receiving what arrives. Reception is the first composite built from two distinct primes: 6 = 2 × 3. The Reception of Origin picks up at the first moment the grammar holds what it has traversed toward.

End of Chapter 5 — The Action of Origin.
