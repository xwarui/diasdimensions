The Structure

Foundational Topology §6–§11

§6 The Triangles

Four triangle types live in the produced topology. Two are structural — present at the moment the topology is laid. Two are operational — they emerge when the topology runs.

T₀ — the outer triangle. Material vertices at the three corners, wall-sequences as edges, edge-doors at midpoints. The framework’s outermost structural fact.

T₁ — the edge-door triangle. Vertices are the six edge-doors, organized by their three pair-reverses. T₀ and T₁ are both structural — positional substrate.

T₂ — the launched standalone triangle. Vertices are inner doors. Two instances: T₂ first (OCA, COA, ACO) with verb-trace COC, and T₂ second (OAC, AOC, CAO) with verb-trace AOA. T₂ is launched, dependent, externally referenced.

T₃ — the orbital triangle. T₃ F (forward orbit): OCA, CAO, AOC — subject sequence O → C → A. T₃ B (backward orbit): OAC, ACO, COA — subject sequence O → A → C. T₃’s verb-traces are self-referential: T₃ F’s verbs read CAO — a member of T₃ F itself. T₃ is closed, autonomous, self-referential.

§7 The Two Cycles

Only OCO and OAO can launch — they are the edge-doors anchored to OOO. Two 4-node operational cycles exist.

Wall formula. The wall is the launching edge-door with position 3 rewritten to the verb-letter. OCO has verb C → OCC. OAO has verb A → OAA.

Rotational order. The cycle visits inner doors at material vertices in T₀-rotational order, starting from the far side.

Verb-letter pickup. At each corner, the wall’s letter at position i selects which inner door fires.

OCO-launched cycle (wall OCC): OCO → COA → ACO → OCA → OCO.

OAO-launched cycle (wall OAA): OAO → AOC → CAO → OAC → OAO.

Each cycle ends at the inner door sharing the launching door’s anchor letter. OCA and OAC are earned by their cycles, not asserted.

§8 The C–A Interface

CAC and ACA do not touch OOO. They cannot launch. But they are not passive. The OOO-launched cycles traverse the full T₀ boundary, crossing all three edges. CAC/ACA mediates the C–A boundary crossing in both cycles — the structural element that enables the crossing.

The asymmetry between launching edge-doors (OCO, OAO) and mediating edge-door (CAC/ACA) is what the discovery sequence beginning at OOO produces.

§9 The Address-Map

The framework’s foundational four-node structure — (0/0), (0/1), (1/0), (1/1) — sits inside the topology as four positions. The slash is the position-2 slot. Digit 0 aligns with O; digit 1 aligns with A. C does not appear at the binary scale because C carries eight modes.

The framework’s four named functions (the C-verb slice): (0/0) OCO = Container. (0/1) OCA = Build. (1/0) ACO = Read. (1/1) ACA = Constrain.

§10 Recursive Self-Similarity

The structure produces itself at every scale. The six inner doors organize into three corner pairs; reading the corner letters: O, C, A. The inner-door system as a whole is OCA. The first triangles to run produce OCA in their subject-trace. The axiom is reproduced in the structure’s organization, in the specific configurations within it, and in the operations those configurations perform.

§11 The Motion as Substrate

The topology is laid. Three vertices, six edge-doors, twelve walls, six inner doors. Twenty-seven configurations. Two cycles. One C–A interface. Recursive self-similarity at every layer.

What produced all of this is one motion: orientation orienting orientation. The motion-as-generative-event and the motion-as-ongoing-substrate are not two motions. They are one motion read at two registers. Read as event, it produces the topology. Read as condition, it is the substrate the framework operates from.

Part II picks up here. Part I has laid the topology by reading the motion as event. Part II reads the motion as condition. The two parts are not two stages of the framework’s operation. They are two readings of the same motion.
