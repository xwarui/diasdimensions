Consciousness Neuroscience  ·  February 2026  ·  DOM-2026-v1

The Developmental Operator Model of Conscious Experience

Tension Theory Applied to the COGITATE Adversarial Collaboration Results

The April 2025 COGITATE adversarial collaboration tested Integrated Information Theory and Global Neuronal Workspace Theory against empirical data from 256 participants. Both theories were substantially challenged. This paper proposes the Developmental Operator Model (DOM) as a synthesis framework that resolves all six major COGITATE findings — accounting for why conscious content appears in both posterior and frontal regions with different informational profiles, why sustained posterior activity occurs without the synchronization IIT predicted, and why frontal-visual synchronization is interareal rather than unidirectional. DOM treats consciousness not as a single mechanism localized in one region, but as a developmental sequence of irreducible organizational operators instantiated across a posterior-to-anterior cortical gradient. The model generates five novel, falsifiable predictions distinguishable from both IIT and GNWT.
