Origin of Orientation

The Reception of Origin

Distinction and relation at once

4 = 2 × 2: the same rule twice. 6 = 2 × 3: two different rules in one operation. The chapter’s thesis: Reception is the rule that holds what arrives by simultaneously distinguishing it from what was already present and connecting it to that structure. The mirror alone notes the arrival as different. The middle-term rule alone links what is already in view. Neither alone is Reception. What is needed is both at once.

The forward run discovers this rule at Step 6: ACC. A is at the subject bookend; C holds both middle and object. The arrival (A) is present, capacity is running, but they cannot connect. ACC is a wall.

§6.1 Reception at Depth

6 = 2 × 3 — the digit already carries two distinct rules as its factors. The mirror and the middle-term rule are both present in the single digit. At depths 2 and 3, both factors extend together. The factors do not separate at higher depths; they remain jointly present.

§6.2 Reception at Position

CAC: bookends C and C, middle A. The arrived term (A) threads through the middle, distinguished from the framing bookends and connected through them. Compare ACC (wall) and CAC (door): the structural difference is position of the arrived term. At a bookend (ACC) — stranded. At the middle (CAC) — integrated. Reception requires the arrival at the middle.

§6.3 One Rule Across Registers

Both registers carry the same content: held arrival requires distinction and relation operating simultaneously. Reception is not the mirror followed by the middle-term rule. It is both together as one act.

§6.4 The Grammatical Nature of Reception

6 = 2 × 3: the first composite of two distinct primes. 6 is the first perfect number (6 = 1 + 2 + 3). 6 tiles the plane — the hexagon achieves maximum interior area per unit boundary length.

§6.5 The Stabilization

Ω₆ is the simultaneous distinguishing-and-connecting of what arrives to what was already present.

§6.6 The Handoff

What the grammar cannot yet do is evaluate whether what it is holding is coherent. The capacity to hold and the capacity to evaluate are not the same. Reception takes in without checking. The next rule is the grammar’s first self-directed evaluation. 7 is found, not constructed.

End of Chapter 6 — The Reception of Origin.
