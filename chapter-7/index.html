The Reflection of Origin

Reading back through accumulated output

7, like 2, 3, and 5, is irreducible. The chapter’s thesis: Reflection is the rule by which the grammar reads back through its accumulated output to find whether it resolves. Not receiving new input. Not producing new output. Reading back.

The forward run discovers this rule at Step 7: AAO. A holds both subject and middle; O is stranded at the object bookend. The grammar is on the return arc, close to origin, but the channel does not open. AAO is a wall.

§7.1 Reflection at Depth

At depth 2, 77 is the read-back rule applied to itself — the grammar evaluating its own evaluations. 7’s position in the sequence matters: it arrives after five accumulated operations. There is nothing to read back through at depth 1 of Ω₂; there is a full operational history to read back through at depth 1 of Ω₇.

§7.2 Reflection at Position

COC: bookends C and C, middle O. Orientation threads through the middle, connecting the two capacity-ends. Compare OCO (Distinction) and COC (Reflection): OCO has orientation framing capacity — the grammar examining what it can do. COC has capacity framing orientation — the grammar running its substrate through everything capacity built, finding what resolves. The first prime and the last prime are paired on the same edge. Distinction fired on the way out; Reflection fires on the way home.

§7.3 One Rule Across Registers

Reflection is irreducible. What is unique to Ω₇ among the primes: it turns the grammar on itself. Ω₂ distinguishes. Ω₃ connects. Ω₅ traverses. Ω₇ evaluates. Only Ω₇ takes accumulated output as its object.

§7.4 The Grammatical Nature of Reflection

7 is the first prime after the second composite. 7 is the last prime in the operator range — 8 and 9 are both composite. No new irreducible rule enters after Reflection. 7 generates no composite within the range (7×2=14).

§7.5 The Stabilization

Ω₇ is the reading back through accumulated output to find what holds and what does not; trusting what resolves, releasing what creates noise.

§7.6 The Handoff

Reflection produces a sorted field: what resolved and what did not. What the grammar cannot yet do is organize it — arrange what resolved into a structure that can carry forward efficiently. The next composite is 8 = 2³.

End of Chapter 7 — The Reflection of Origin.
